Tables

This section contains various tables that you can use while both running or planning a D&D session. A random encounter table which can be used if you need a quick unique encounter to throw at the party is available. There is also tables that can be used to create names for NPC's, or to generate random loot for the party. As my recommendation, I suggest letting the players roll the dice for these tables. Sometimes you can mix it up and roll them your self but it's always fun to see your players roll their demise.

Random Encounters
d10 Encounter
1 A dragon flies overhead and sees the party, and engages combat.
2 2d6 wolves attack the party.
3 A magical rock begins to glow slightly off the road.
4 A ransacked cart on the road, with a blood trail leading into the woods.
5 A traveling merchant along the road.
6 A small creature peers at the party through the trees. Following it leads to treasure.
7 An abandoned building can be seen just off the path. Within lies a few skeletons and some chests with items.
8 The party encounters a monk on a delivery to a nearby city.
9 A group of 3 ominous horsemen trot past the party. Should they choose to engage the soldiers remain quite and mysterious.
10 The weather worsens. In the foggy distance a shadow holding a lantern waves the party down. Following this light leads them into the forest where they find a small cabin. Within is an older gentleman who offers them food. He asks what the party seeks in their adventure. He asks to come along for the journey. If he comes a quest will follow later.
